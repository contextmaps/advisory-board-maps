Prompt Library

This library is designed to help advisory board members and departmental leaders explore the curriculum dataset, equivalence graph, semantic maps, and geometric embedding space through strong natural-language prompts.

📋 Click the copy button next to any prompt to copy it to your clipboard. Then paste it directly into the GPT prompt window.

1. Explore Course Alignment

Use these prompts to surface the courses most strongly aligned with the employer-input criteria, compare institutions, and identify distinctive strengths in the curriculum.

These prompts rank the strongest courses for a selected criterion, either across the entire dataset or within a specific subset such as VT.

  • •Show me the top 10 courses for map 1.1.
  • •Show me the top 10 VT courses for map 2.5.
  • •Show me the top 15 non-VT courses for map 1.6.
  • •Which courses align most strongly with Enterprise Solution Architecture & Engineering?
  • •Which courses align most strongly with Integrating AI Agents with External Tools and Enterprise Systems?

These prompts show which universities are most visible in the strongest courses for each criterion and support cross-school comparison.

  • •Which universities appear most often in the top 25 courses for map 1.4?
  • •Which universities have the strongest courses in Networking Fundamentals?
  • •Compare VT, MIT, and CMU for map 1.5.
  • •Which schools have the strongest representation in AI Systems and Autonomous Agents?
  • •Show me the universities with the highest average nonzero scores in map 2.1.

These prompts focus attention on VT courses that stand out semantically across one or more employer criteria.

  • •Which VT courses align most strongly with map 1.6?
  • •Which VT courses have the highest MAX_MAP_SCORE?
  • •Which VT courses are strongly aligned with one criterion but not others?
  • •Which VT courses appear repeatedly across multiple maps?
  • •Show me VT courses with nonzero scores in both map 1.4 and map 2.3.

These prompts distinguish broadly relevant courses from narrowly specialized ones using the 16 semantic map columns and the MAX_MAP_SCORE summary.

  • •Which courses are narrowly specialized in one map?
  • •Which courses have nonzero scores across the largest number of maps?
  • •Which courses are broadly relevant across technical and professional skill areas?
  • •Which courses have the highest score in one map but little presence elsewhere?
  • •Compare the map profiles of BIT_3444 and BIT_4464.

2. Explore Equivalences and Curriculum Structure

Use these prompts to explore VT-centered equivalence relationships, identify peer institutions, and benchmark the structural reach of specific courses or thematic areas.

These prompts retrieve peer courses linked to VT courses through the equivalence graph using the EQUIVALENCE column.

  • •Show me the courses equivalent to BIT_4464.
  • •Which universities have courses equivalent to BIT_3444?
  • •How many equivalent courses does BIT_4464 have?
  • •List all equivalent courses for BIT_2406.
  • •Show the titles and universities of courses equivalent to BIT_3554.

These prompts compare a selected VT course with the peer courses attached to it through the equivalence graph.

  • •Compare BIT_4464 with its equivalent courses.
  • •Which equivalent courses to BIT_3444 also score highly in map 1.4?
  • •Among the courses equivalent to BIT_4464, which have the highest MAX_MAP_SCORE?
  • •Which equivalent courses to BIT_2406 belong to DSS?
  • •Compare the descriptions of BIT_4464 and its equivalent peer courses.

These prompts help identify where VT has broad peer counterparts, where it appears sparse, and where peer institutions seem to have stronger thematic coverage.

  • •Which VT courses have many peer equivalents?
  • •Which VT courses have few or no peer equivalents?
  • •Which universities appear most often in VT equivalence matches?
  • •Which curricular areas have strong peer coverage but weaker VT representation?
  • •Where does VT appear distinctive relative to peer institutions?

These prompts translate the equivalence graph into benchmark-style questions about VT’s structural position among peer schools.

  • •Which universities most often provide equivalents to VT’s analytics courses?
  • •Which VT courses in the DSS track have the broadest peer equivalence coverage?
  • •Which maps have strong semantic relevance but weak equivalence coverage?
  • •Which VT courses appear structurally isolated in the equivalence graph?
  • •Which institutions seem closest to VT in course equivalence patterns?

3. Explore Spatial and Visual Patterns

Use these prompts to interrogate the embedding geometry directly, combine spatial proximity with semantic relevance, and create visual summaries on demand.

These prompts use the tSNE or UMAP coordinates to identify local neighborhoods, nearby universities, and potential peer clusters around a selected course.

  • •Show me the 10 courses closest to BIT_4464 in UMAP space.
  • •Show me the 10 courses closest to BIT_3444 in tSNE space.
  • •Which universities appear near BIT_4464 in the UMAP map?
  • •Which courses are spatial neighbors of BIT_3444 but are not equivalence matches?
  • •Show all courses within a small UMAP radius of BIT_4464.

These prompts combine map scores with geometric proximity to show where semantic alignment and local embedding structure reinforce each other.

  • •Show courses near BIT_3444 that also score highly in map 1.3.
  • •Which nearby courses to BIT_4464 also appear in map 2.1?
  • •Among the spatial neighbors of BIT_3444, which have the highest MAX_MAP_SCORE?
  • •Which courses are close in UMAP space and equivalent in the graph?
  • •Show courses near BIT_4464 that align with cloud security.

These prompts produce straightforward ranked visualizations such as line and bar charts for a selected criterion.

  • •Plot the top 20 similarity scores for map 2.5 as a descending line chart.
  • •Create a bar chart of the top 15 courses for map 1.1.
  • •Show the top 10 VT courses for map 1.6 as a column chart.
  • •Plot the top 20 courses in map 2.1 with course numbers on the x-axis and scores on the y-axis.
  • •Create a ranked bar chart of the strongest AI-related courses.

These prompts create side-by-side comparisons across institutions, groups, or map families using grouped and multi-line displays.

  • •Create a clustered column chart comparing VT, MIT, and CMU across maps 1.1 to 1.6.
  • •Compare the average nonzero scores of selected universities across the 16 maps.
  • •Show a multi-line plot of VT, MIT, and Indiana across all map categories.
  • •Create a grouped bar chart of the number of nonzero courses per university for maps in Technical Skills.
  • •Compare DSS, OSM, and CMA coverage for VT across the 16 maps.

These prompts turn the 16 semantic map columns into compact course profiles that can be compared visually using radar charts.

  • •Create a radar plot of BIT_4464 across all 16 maps.
  • •Compare BIT_3444 and BIT_4464 using a radar chart.
  • •Create a radar plot for the strongest VT course in each map.
  • •Compare the profile of BIT_4464 with its equivalent peer courses.
  • •Show which maps dominate the profile of a selected course.

These prompts create dense visual summaries across courses, institutions, or map categories to reveal larger patterns in the data.

  • •Create a heatmap of the top 50 courses across all 16 maps.
  • •Show a university-by-map heatmap using average nonzero scores.
  • •Create a heatmap of VT courses across the 16 maps.
  • •Show which institutions are strongest across the AI Systems and Autonomous Agents maps.
  • •Create a heatmap comparing DSS, OSM, and CMA courses across the criteria.

4. Strategic Questions for Advisory Board Discussion

Use these prompts to frame higher-level conversations around curriculum strengths, gaps, positioning, and opportunities for future development.

These prompts support discussion of what the current curriculum appears to do well relative to the employer criteria.

  • •What are VT’s strongest courses relative to the employer criteria?
  • •Which employer criteria are best represented by the current BIT curriculum?
  • •Which courses should be highlighted to the Advisory Board as distinctive strengths?
  • •Which VT courses appear most repeatedly in the strongest relevance results across the maps?

These prompts are designed to surface possible underrepresented areas or criteria where peer institutions appear stronger.

  • •Which employer criteria show limited VT coverage?
  • •Where do peer universities appear stronger than VT?
  • •Which maps have strong peer representation but only modest VT presence?
  • •Which topic areas look like the clearest opportunities for curriculum expansion?

These prompts help frame VT’s overall curricular identity relative to peers and identify where it appears especially close or distinctive.

  • •Which universities seem most similar to VT overall?
  • •Which courses make VT look distinctive?
  • •Which map areas suggest future curriculum development opportunities?
  • •How does VT compare to MIT and Indiana across the main employer skill sets?

These prompts look for bridge courses, multi-map connectors, and cross-cutting patterns that span technical, AI, and professional categories.

  • •Which courses bridge technical, AI, and professional skill areas?
  • •Which courses serve as connectors across multiple employer criteria?
  • •Which curriculum areas appear broad versus specialized?
  • •Which courses are both semantically strong and structurally well connected?
